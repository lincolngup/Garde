Why was the BuckleGarde developed?

In 2007, a patient died after unbuckling from a gurney, opening the door of a moving ambulance, and departing from the back of the vehicle. Fellow University of Denver MBA students Wayne Guerra, an EMS physician, and Lincoln Gup, a former EMT, had both seen patients and paramedic personnel injured after patients suddenly and surprisingly unbuckled themselves from their safety straps. The potentially preventable ambulance tragedy, coupled with their collective experiences, motivated them to find a way to make patient transports safer. To achieve this goal, Wayne and Lincoln started Garde Systems LLC.

Four-point restraints and similar systems can prevent patients from exiting an ambulance by themselves, but these kinds of restraints can cause psychological damage and even physical harm as patients struggle against them. Rather than going this direction, Wayne and Lincoln felt they needed to create a way to make it very difficult for patients to unbuckle the gurney safety belt. They developed the BuckleGarde as a safety buckle cover that can be quickly manipulated by healthcare personnel, but is very difficult for patients to unbuckle themselves. This created a new level of restraint that was more secure than verbal instructions but short of physically restraining the arms or legs.

How was the BuckleGarde developed?

While the concept is simple, it took over 30 computer-aided design (CAD) drawings and seven prototypes for us to arrive at our finished product. Each version was shown to practicing paramedics and their recommendations were put into subsequent modifications until we arrived at a product that was both versatile and functional.

What's the BuckleGarde made out of?

We chose an advanced plastic technology with the physical characteristics to withstand demanding use. Similar to the material used in some fire helmets, the BuckleGarde’s plastic offers excellent resistance to wear, heat, and chemicals.

What straps does the BuckleGarde work with?

Our customers have helped us evolve by giving us the opportunity to meet a wide variety of application challenges. The BuckleGarde is designed to accommodate both a safety buckle and shoulder straps. We’ve tested the BuckleGarde with hundreds of combinations of safety buckles and straps.

How many BuckleGardes should we put on a cot?

The user is responsible for evaluating and determining how many BuckleGardes are suitable and appropriate for a particular use and intended application.
